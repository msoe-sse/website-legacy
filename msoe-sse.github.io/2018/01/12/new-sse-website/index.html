Welcome to the New SSE Website

12 January 2018 by Hunter Parks

I'm glad you're here! We have some amazing stuff going on.

Over the past year, MSOE's Society of Software Engineers has been busy working on updating their website to make it more flexible, informative, and open. One of the biggest motivators behind this movement is to allow student members of SSE to actively participate in the development and maintenance of this website.

Originally, the official SSE website was going to be created using Angular. As time went on, it was clear that the open source blogging platform Ghost might be a better choice for SSE.

What is Ghost?

Ghost is a fully open source, hackable platform for building and running a modern online publication. We power blogs, magazines and journalists from Zappos to Sky News.

In other terms, Ghost is a publication platform that can be leveraged to accomplish many different site goals - and it is open source! This means that SSE can use the platform to build an engaging, informative, and custom website that can be programmed by the students of SSE.

What is in it for me?

As an SSE member, this website will your hub for all things SSE. From this site you will be able to see calendar events, interesting articles in the world of technology and engineering, and participate in discussion with fellow SSE members.

Can I help?

Of course! The purpose of this website is to allow students to grow and learn in their programming skills. While some knowledge of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript can come in handy, no prior knowledge is required - we would love to teach you! All SSE website source code and content is available on our Github repository.

About SSE

The Society of Software Engineers is a professional organization built to improve the experience of students enrolled in the Software Engineering program at the Milwaukee School of Engineering by providing opportunities for collaboration, further education and guidance for new and old students, and representation among other departments at the university.

We focus on building you professionally, which includes improving you as a person and as a coder. Our events and community are designed to help every member reach their fullest potential.
